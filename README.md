Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)
•	CLO1: Employ a range of photographic techniques and demonstrate creative applications of these techniques.
•	CLO2: Demonstrate a critical and creative approach to image making through a series of experimental images.
•	CLO3: Communicate visually and textually demonstrating critical consideration of key issues in contemporary photography.
•	CLO4: Demonstrate an understanding of the role that key photographers have played in the evolution of photography.
•	CLO5: Demonstrate a unique and individual approach toward creative photography through an experimental process.

Pedagogical Framework: "rễ-root"
The course is unified by the "rễ-root" framework, drawing from Vietnamese ("rễ" meaning root) and Eastern philosophical traditions. This framework invites students to explore identity as complex networks:
•	Visible roots and hidden roots (what we show vs. what we conceal)
•	Individual roots and collective roots (personal vs. communal identity)
•	Inherited roots and chosen roots (given heritage vs. constructed belonging)
The framework manifests in the final exhibition theme and the online exhibition website: binyoun.github.io/reroot/

Diverse Learner Support Strategies
Implementing Universal Design for Learning principles (Rose & Meyer, 2002) and differentiated instruction (Tomlinson, 2014):
•	Differentiated Pathways: Option A (installation-based) vs. Option B (slide-based) for A2 Final Project
•	Flexible Scope: 10-30 images allows students to choose depth vs. breadth based on conceptual approach
•	HD Exemplars: Previous semester examples shared during Workshop Part 1
•	Multiple Consultation Modes: In-person (Miro booking), online (Teams), email support
•	Scaffolded Conceptual Development: Ideation worksheets, "From Reference to Direction" workshop (Week 10)
•	Cross-Disciplinary Peer Learning: Business students assist Design students with statements; Design students guide Business students on spatial arrangement
•	Culturally Responsive References: Vietnamese (Duy-Phuong Le Nguyen, Pham Tuan Ngoc) and Korean (Tuna Lee, Ilam Stephen Jang) artists alongside Western references from Robertson and McDaniel (2017)
•	Strategic Break Placement: Week 9 provides independent development time between Workshops 1 and 2
